A top-down farming sim where **you don't play so much as watch AI farmers play**.
A field of BDI-driven farmers plant, harvest, fish, forage, and trade across 100
in-game days, chasing gold. You *can* step in as **Pip** — but the drama is
emergent, not scripted.

## The cast

Four named archetypes are the protagonists, each a distinct deliberation
strategy; the rest of the field are archetype clones (`Cora-0`, `Atticus-1`, …)
on procedurally-generated farm islands.

## How a farmer thinks

Each farmer is a **Belief–Desire–Intention** agent on a daily action-point
budget, running a per-tick `PERCEIVE → ACT` FSM. `DeliberateSystem` scores every
option — farming *and* non-farm work (fishing, foraging, mining) — in the same
**gold-per-AP** unit, emitting an `Intention` queue that `ActSystem` drains.
Skill-gated scoring means ~10 of 21 farmers lean non-farm while farming stays the
most common #1 choice — diversity without hand-authoring.

<Aside type="tip" title="The economy in one line">
  A crop's score is `g_c = (2·P_c − S_c) / (G_c + 2)` — profit per plot over labour.
  The market wall is an async peer-to-peer channel with escrowed offers
  (`OFFER_TTL_DAYS = 3`) and per-personality price gates. Full math on
  [Patterns](/patterns/) and [Economy](/wiki/economy/).
</Aside>

## Pip, the playable farmer

Pip is a real farmer entity — same components as the AI — tagged
`personality.kind: "pip"` plus a `player` component. The only difference:
intentions come from the keyboard, not the deliberator. `PlayerControlSystem`
runs before `ActSystem`; **movement is free** (not AP-gated), but actions still
obey AP, tool, and proximity (Chebyshev ≤1) rules. The whole GUI — inspect card,
drag-from-world hotbar, diegetic HUD — renders through the game-agnostic
`@engine/ui` toolkit.

## How it runs

<Badge text="server-side sim" variant="success" /> &nbsp; <Badge text="WebSocket transport" variant="note" />

Uniquely among the three games, Farm Valley runs its sim **server-side** in a
Node `SimHost`. The browser client opens a WebSocket, receives one
`RenderSnapshot` per tick, and interpolates between the latest two. Input crosses
the socket behind an owner gate — spectators can watch, but can't drive Pip.
