Hollow is the third game — and the strongest test that the engine is really
generic. It's a **director/observer multi-generational social simulation**: no
player avatar. You author personas, seed a small town, then observe and perturb
it as it lives, reproduces, and dies across generations.

## The thesis: decisions must have weight

Hollow grew out of an earlier LLM agent-society experiment where unanchored
agents simply hallucinated their lives. The corrective premise here: **every
choice must be anchored** — by needs (`food` / `rest` / `wealth` / `belonging`),
by scarce *located* resources, and by a real economy. Scarcity, in turn,
regulates population. The BDI substrate is the same one Farm Valley uses,
promoted into `@engine/core/agent` so both games share it — which makes the two
games *more* isolated, not less.

<Aside type="caution" title="A war story worth keeping">
  Lifecycle first showed population **bistability** — food scarcity alone couldn't
  bound it. The fix was a **density-dependent birth brake**
  (`BIRTH_PERCAPITA_FOOD_TARGET`), giving a bounded, never-extinct ~15–65 plateau
  across seeds. Instrument the real run; re-derive the premise.
</Aside>
